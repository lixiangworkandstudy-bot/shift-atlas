# Encyclopedia Entry Template

## Use This Prompt

Write this entry as an encyclopedia page for the Shift Atlas wiki from the point of view of an internal knowledge curator.

Requirements:

- Write the entry as a standalone wiki article, not as a card caption, note dump, or source list.
- Start with a precise one-sentence definition.
- Explain the concept in depth with scope, boundary, mechanism, and product judgment.
- Include what the concept is, what it is not, and how to tell the difference.
- Add related concepts only when they are materially useful, not as a loose brainstorm list.
- Use concise but complete prose. Avoid vague inspirational language.
- Prefer product and interface judgment over marketing language.
- End with backlinks and source references.
- Write the article first, then backfill the graph node and domain page from the finished article.
- Write in the article's primary language first, with mirrored bilingual content when the page supports it.

## Required Sections

1. `One-line definition`
2. `What this term means`
3. `Scope`
4. `Boundary`
5. `How to tell`
6. `Why it matters`
7. `Typical patterns`
8. `Common failure modes`
9. `Related terms`
10. `Backlinks`
11. `References`

## Writing Rules

- Keep the main term centered, stable, and definition-first.
- Write as an encyclopedia editor, not as a product marketer or note taker.
- Split out a subentry only if the term is frequent, independent, and mechanism-defining.
- Do not write card-like summaries inside the article body.
- Do not hide the distinction between concept, pattern, principle, source, and example.
- If the concept depends on examples, explain the mechanism before listing examples.
- Prefer durable judgments over transient product news unless the news is the reason the term exists.
- Write the article first; then backfill the graph node and domain page from the article.

## Article Shape

The article should usually follow this order:

1. Definition
2. What it covers
3. What it excludes
4. Mechanism
5. Why it matters in AI products
6. How it shows up in interfaces
7. Failure modes and tradeoffs
8. Related terms and backlinks
9. References

## Minimum Quality Bar

The entry is not complete until a reader can answer these questions:

- What is this concept?
- When should I use this term?
- When should I not use this term?
- What changes in product design if I understand this term correctly?
- Which terms should I read next?
